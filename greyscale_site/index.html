Objectives

Where are people going?

In a globalized world where international communication and ease of travel have become commonplace, migration plays a huge role in shaping human activity. In the United States, this article indicates that Florida, Texas, and California are the most popular states, respectively, for new residents. We want to analyze whether this holds in line with the US Government Census Bureau data, and whether this trend changes over a period of time.

Florida

In addition to warm weather throughout most of the year. Florida is also one of few states without a state income tax and generally lower cost of living, depending on the city. Combined with world-renowned theme parks, stunning beaches, and historic towns, Florida is a top candidate for those seeking a new home.

Texas

This site states that "affordability, jobs, and delicious food are only just the tip of the iceberg" in the list of the many reasons to move to Texas. Indeed, this state boasts no state income tax or corporate income tax, a lower-than-national-average unemployment rate, several universities, and emerging culinary and cultural scenes throughout the state.

California

It's no secret California is the hub of the tech industry. However, despite a staggering number of people moving out of the state, California continues to attract a steady migration ofnew residents who enjoy the state's natural beauty, Mediterranean weather, better paying jobs and more advanced technology.
